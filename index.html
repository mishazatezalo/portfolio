LionLink

Why?

LionLink was created to address a significant problem many students face on large university campuses: loneliness and difficulty forming connections in a sea of over 50,000 people. With so many students and events happening simultaneously, it can be overwhelming and isolating for students to find a sense of belonging. LionLink‚Äôs goal is to provide a safe, engaging platform where students can discover events, meet new people, and form meaningful connections. By focusing on creating a digital space that fosters community, LionLink aims to solve the problem of campus isolation and encourage social engagement.

UX/UI Design

From a UI/UX design perspective, the name LionLink was chosen to reflect the university‚Äôs mascot and symbolize connection between students. The color scheme was crafted with soft yet energetic tones‚Äîblending school pride with a welcoming atmosphere. The layout was designed to be simple and user-friendly, ensuring that students can easily browse events, connect with peers, and explore campus activities without feeling overwhelmed. Full-Stack Implementation

On the full-stack engineering side, I used React for the front-end and Node.js with MongoDB for the back-end to create a scalable platform that could handle hundreds of users concurrently. The platform features secure user authentication and real-time updates for events, allowing students to interact with live data and stay up-to-date with campus activities. I also implemented a web scraper to automate event data collection, reducing manual input by 80%. The seamless integration between the front-end and back-end ensured fast load times, smooth interactions, and a reliable platform, allowing LionLink to deliver on its promise of fostering connection in a large university setting.
